# Theanine
*Source: https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Theanine*

## Dosage & Duration

### Oral

**Dosage:**
- Threshold: 50 mg
- Light: 75 - 175 mg
- Common: 175 - 300 mg
- Strong: 300 - 500 mg
- Heavy: 500 mg +

**Duration:**
- Total: 3 - 6 hours[1]
- Onset: 30 - 90 minutes[1]
- Peak: 1 - 3 hours
- After effects: 6 - 12 hours

**L-Theanine** (also known as **Theanine** , **L-γ-glutamylethylamide** and **N5-ethyl-L-glutamine** ) is an analog of the amino acids of [glutamate](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Glutamate) and glutamine. It was discovered as a constituent of green tea in 1949 and in 1950 was isolated from gyokuro leaves, which have high theanine content.

The appearance of the name "theanine" without a prefix is understood to imply the L- [enantiomer](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Enantiomer) , which is the form found in fresh teas and in some, but not all dietary supplements. The opposite D-enantiomer has far less studied pharmacologic properties, but is present in racemic chemical preparations, and substantially in some studied theanine supplements.

This compound is used as a [nootropic](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Nootropic) for its calming and relaxing properties. It is often taken in combination with [caffeine](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Caffeine) as it has been shown to mitigate its negative aspects, such as [anxiety](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Anxiety) , [increased blood pressure](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Increased_blood_pressure) and [diminished sleep quality](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Wakefulness) , while possibly improving upon the positive aspects. Its ability to enhance attention has been repeatedly verified.

A recent systematic review of the effects of theanine and caffeine has confirmed that the combination seems to improve aspects of attention. The combination of L-theanine and caffeine may improve attention more than caffeine alone.

## Chemistry

Theanine, or N-ethyl-L-glutamine, is an amino acid analogue of L-glutamine. Its structure is comprised of a five carbon straight chain carboxylic acid called pentanoic acid, which is bonded to an amino group at R 2 , and an additional ketone group at R 5 . Also substituted at R 5 of the pentanoic group is an ethylamino chain connected at its amino constituent. Theanine is understood to refer to the levorotary enantiomer, which is well documented, rather than the relatively unresearched dextrorotary enantiomer.

## Pharmacology

Theanine is structurally similar to the excitatory [neurotransmitter](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Neurotransmitter) [glutamate](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Glutamate) , and in accordance, binds to glutamate [receptors](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Receptor) , though with much lower affinity in comparison. Specifically, it binds to ionotropic glutamate receptors including the [AMPA](/w/index.php?title=AMPA&action=edit&redlink=1) and [kainate](/w/index.php?title=Kainate&action=edit&redlink=1) receptors and, to a lesser extent, the [NMDA](/w/index.php?title=NMDA&action=edit&redlink=1) receptor. It acts as an [antagonist](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Antagonist) of the former two sites and as an [agonist](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Agonist) of the latter site. In addition, it inhibits glutamine transporters and glutamate transporters, and thus acts as [reuptake inhibitor](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Reuptake_inhibitor) of glutamine and glutamate.

Theanine increases [dopamine](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Dopamine) , [GABA](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/GABA) , and [glycine](/w/index.php?title=Glycine&action=edit&redlink=1) levels in various areas of the brain. It also affects [serotonin](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Serotonin) in a manner which is still a matter of debate in the scientific community, with separate studies showing increases and decreases in brain serotonin levels using similar experimental protocols.

These various changes in neurotransmitter levels contribute to the calming and nootropic properties of theanine.

### Physical effects
 
- - **[Sedation](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Sedation)** & **[Stimulation](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Stimulation)** - Theanine is mildly stimulating at [light](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Dosage#light) to [common](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Dosage#common) dosages but becomes sedating starting with [strong doses](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Dosage#Strong) at around 300mg.
- **[Muscle relaxation](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Muscle_relaxation)** - At [heavy](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Dosage#Heavy) doses, theanine induces muscle relaxation that is vaguely comparable to that of the relaxation produced by [benzodiazepines](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Benzodiazepines) .
- **[Spontaneous bodily sensations](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Spontaneous_bodily_sensations)** - Theanine can produce a physical feeling of glowing warmth that is present throughout one's body at higher doses.
- **[Headaches](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Headaches)** - Headaches are usually only reported at high doses, well above 200mg. ### Cognitive effects
 
- - **[Anxiety suppression](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Anxiety_suppression)** - Theanine produces anxiolytic effects which are comparable to a mild dose of a benzodiazepine but without the accompanying [disinhibition](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Disinhibition) .
- **[Mindfulness](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Mindfulness)** - Some users report that theanine can produce uniquely distinct states of mindfulness.
- **[Memory enhancement](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Memory_enhancement)** - Theanine has been shown to improve memory and attention in subjects with mild cognitive impairments.
- **[Addiction suppression](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Addiction_suppression)** - Some anecdotal reports suggest that theanine may mitigate some of the effects of [opioid withdrawal](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Opioid_withdrawal) , aswell as withdrawal from GABAergic substances like [alcohol](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Alcohol) and [benzodiazepines](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Benzodiazepines)

- **[Analysis enhancement](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Analysis_enhancement)**
- **[Focus enhancement](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Focus_enhancement)**

## Toxicity and harm potential

Theanine is non-addictive, is not known to cause brain damage, and has an extremely low toxicity relative to dose. [*[citation needed](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Citation_needed)*] There are relatively few physical side effects associated with acute theanine exposure. Various studies have shown that in reasonable doses in a careful context, it presents no negative cognitive, psychiatric or toxic physical consequences. [*[citation needed](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Citation_needed)*]

It is strongly recommended that one use [harm reduction practices](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Responsible_drug_use) when using this substance.

### Tolerance and addiction potential

Theanine is not habit-forming.

Tolerance to the effects of theanine are built up after prolonged and repeated usage. After noticeable tolerance has been built, it takes about 5 days for tolerance to be reduced by half and 10 days return to baseline. Theanine presents cross-tolerance with no other known compounds, meaning that after the consumption of theanine all other psychoactive compounds will not have a reduced effect.

## Legal status

Theanine is freely available to possess and distribute and is approved in most countries as a dietary supplement.

- **Canada** Theanine is available as a dietary supplement and is freely available to possess and distribute in Canada. [*[citation needed](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Citation_needed)*]
