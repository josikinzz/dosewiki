# Cocoa
*Source: https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Cocoa*

[Template:SubstanceBox/Cocoa](/w/index.php?title=Template:SubstanceBox/Cocoa&action=edit&redlink=1)

**Cocoa** , or *cocoa powder* is a naturally occurring product from the cocoa beans of the cacao tree *[Theobroma cacao](/w/index.php?title=Theobroma_cacao_(botany)&action=edit&redlink=1)* , known for being a main ingredient in chocolate. It is found usually in the form of cocoa beans, which are taken from the cocoa plant, or cocoa powder. Cocoa beans and bean powder contain psychoactive compounds that when ingested or insufflated act as mild to moderate stimulants. Businesses as well as companies and their subsidiaries sell cocoa powder for baking because cocoa powder is used to add chocolate flavor to baked items or doughs.

The generic name for *Theobroma cacao* is derived from the Greek for "food of the gods"; from θεός (theos), meaning 'god', and βρῶμα (broma), meaning 'food'.

## History and culture

Several mixtures of cacao are described in ancient texts, for ceremonial or medicinal, as well as culinary, purposes. Some mixtures included maize, chili, vanilla (Vanilla planifolia), and honey. Archaeological evidence for use of cacao, while relatively sparse, has come from the recovery of whole cacao beans at Uaxactun, Guatemala.

Ceremonial cacao, along with retreats centered around its use, has been experiencing a surge in popularity in recent years.

## Pharmacology

The compounds theobromine, [caffeine](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Caffeine) , and [phenethylamine](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Phenethylamine) are the main psychoactive constituents of cocoa. Caffeine and theobromine are metabolized by xanthine oxidase and phenethylamine is metabolized mainly by monoamine oxidase B.

Theobromine has a half-life of 10 hours but over 16% may be unmodified 48 h after a single dose of 10 mg/Kg.

It may take 4-8 weeks of caffeine abstinence to reset tolerance in regular users.

## Toxicity and harm potential

It is recommended that one use [harm reduction practices](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Responsible_use) when using this drug. Although cocoa is relatively safe when ingested orally, snorting cocoa is very damaging to nasal passages and therefore it is not recommended to snort cocoa powder often, in large amounts, or potentially even at all.

Snorted cocoa has been used as a "party drug" in Europe, usually as a legal and subtle replacement for [MDMA](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/MDMA) or [cocaine](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Cocaine) . Cocoa solids is a very bulky substance. Insufflation of cocoa solids in the respiratory tract can cause inflammation.

### Overdose

The LD 50 of cocoa powder in humans is estimated to be around 25-100 ***(This information has a large range of numbers (ex. 25-100) that is unjustified or unsourced and/or it is a very vague statement.)*** grams per kilogram of body weight. [*[citation needed](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Citation_needed)*]

### Dependence and abuse potential

Because cocoa can act as a powerful (compared to other weaker stimulants on their own) stimulant, it can be considered addictive, but less so than compounds such as [amphetamine](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Amphetamine) and [DOx](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/DOx) compounds. Caffeine dependence and withdrawals have been documented.

### Dangerous interactions

[monoamine oxidase inhibitors](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/MAOI) or [reversible inhibitors of monoamine oxidase](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/RIMA) could potentially boost the actions of phenethylamine [*[citation needed](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Citation_needed)*] or, more dangerously, raise levels of [monoamines](https://psychonautwiki.org/wiki/Monoamine) to very high amounts which could cause high blood pressure or less potentially, serotonin syndrome.

## Legality

Cocoa powder can be legally obtained likely in every country, although some countries have restrictions on the sale of caffeine.
