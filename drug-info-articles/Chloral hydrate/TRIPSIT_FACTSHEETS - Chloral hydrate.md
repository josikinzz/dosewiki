# Chloral hydrate
*Source: TripSit Factsheets (tripsit.me)*

## Summary

Chloral Hydrate, a sedative drug discovered in 1832 with a history of recreational use going back to the 19th century. The drug is prescribed rarely for extreme insomnia. There is limited information on the drug's long-term effects on the body. The drug is reported to induce a intoxication comparable to alcohol or benzodiazepines.

## Classification
- **Categories:** depressant, habit-forming
- **Also known as:** chloral-hydrate, aquachloral, somnos

## Dosage

### Oral
- **Common:** 200-500mg
- **Light:** 200mg
- **Strong:** 500-1000mg

## Duration
- **Onset:** 20-60 minutes
- **Duration:** 5-10 hours
- **After Effects:** 2-6 hours

## Additional Information
- **Avoid:** All other CNS depressants.
