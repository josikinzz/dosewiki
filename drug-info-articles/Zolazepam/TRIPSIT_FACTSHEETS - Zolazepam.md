# Zolazepam
*Source: TripSit Factsheets (tripsit.me)*

## Summary

A substance that is structurally related to benzodiazepines. It is only used medically in veterinary medicine. It is usually used in tandem with either the potent NMDA antagonist tiletamine (at a 1:1 ratio) or with the Î±2 adrenergic receptor agonist xylazine. The drug is roughly around four times the potency of diazepam, yet is reportedly water soluable.

## Classification
- **Categories:** benzodiazepine, depressant, habit-forming

## Dosage

### Insufflated
- **Common:** 3-6mg
- **Light:** 1-3mg
- **Strong:** 6-12mg+

### Intramuscular
- **Common:** 3-5mg
- **Light:** 1-3mg
- **Strong:** 5-10mg+

### Oral
- **Common:** 5-10mg
- **Light:** 2.5-5mg
- **Strong:** 10-20mg+

## Duration
- **Onset:** minutes
- **Duration:** hours
- **After Effects:** hours

## Additional Information
- **Avoid:** All other CNS depressants.
